New Recruit

Recruit Access Checking

The Armed Forces Bank Recruit Access Checking account meets your everyday needs as a recruit and gives you the freedom to manage your money with ease.  Sign up for direct deposit and get paid an extra day in advance with Early Pay (DFAS direct deposits).  Enjoy convenient access to online banking features such as free mobile check deposit1, bill pay and account alerts3.  The Recruit Checking Account offers special benefits such as free overdraft protection transfers and free incoming wire transfers.  No minimum balance is required to open your account today.

Some of our nationwide branches offer Exchanges that are open weekends and evenings. Armed Forces Bank clients can also bank at over 70 Academy Bank locations and deposit cash at over 4,000 Walmart locations at the register. In addition, you’ll have free access to 30,000+ ATMs through our MoneyPass Network2.

Features

No minimum balance required

No monthly service charge with direct deposit

Receive DFAS direct deposits a day early with Military Early Pay

No credit check

Free, unlimited access to 30,000+ MoneyPass® Network2 ATMs — find one with our mobile app or the MoneyPass® site

Free mobile check deposit1

Cash2DebitTM - add cash to your account from any Walmart location at the cashier5

Additional Features

  • No fee money orders for existing clients

  • Free overdraft protection transfers

  • Free e.statements or $5 paper statements

  • Digital Banking with Bill Pay and Account Alerts3

  • Free Visa® debit card with EMV chip technology

  • Faster, more secure online transactions when you enroll in Visa® Checkout

  • Locations around the country, including branches inside Exchanges that are open weekends and evenings

What You Need to Know

  • Available to recruits ages 17 and up. No parent or guardian required.
  • No monthly service charge when you make a direct deposit each statement cycle. Otherwise, a $12 monthly service charge applies.
  • Free e.Statements or $5 paper statements. Other miscellaneous fees, including overdraft fees, may also apply.
  • Closing new accounts within 90 days of opening will result in a $20 early closure fee.

Are you a recruiter? See our Recruiter Resources.

How to Open an Account

  • Step 1: Fill out the Signature Card (PDF) and email or fax it back to us with a copy of your ID. View our list of Acceptable Forms of ID (PDF) to open an account.
  • Step 2: We will call you with an account number within minutes! In addition to sending a debit card, we will mail starter checks and other account information. Quick ship? We will overnight a debit card to you!
  • Step 3: Print the Direct Deposit Form (PDF) and take to Basic Training or submit to your Finance Office.

Take The Next Steps

Don’t stop here! Once you get your new checking account, take your finances to the next level:

  • Download the Checking 101 Workbook to learn the ins and outs of your new checking account.
  • Access your account on your desktop, smartphone or tablet with Digital Banking. Enroll today.
  • Move payday up a day with Military Early Pay.
  • Build your credit with a Credit Builder Secured Visa Credit Card.4
  • Open a Savings Account and enroll in Saving Cents, a program that automatically rounds up every purchase to the nearest dollar and puts the change in your savings.
  • Ramp up your financial readiness and check out our Education section in the main menu.
  • If you have any questions, contact our dedicated Recruit Department.

IMPORTANT INFORMATION ABOUT PROCEDURES FOR OPENING A NEW ACCOUNT

To help the government fight the funding of terrorism and money laundering activities, Federal law requires all financial institutions to obtain, verify, and record information that identifies each person who opens an account. What this means for you: When you open an account, we will ask for your name, address, date of birth, and other information that will allow us to identify you. We may also ask to see your driver's license or other identifying documents. Legal Entities may be required to certify ownership and managerial control information.
